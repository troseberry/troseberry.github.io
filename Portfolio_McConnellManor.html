MCCONNELL MANOR

Project Overview

Project Duration:

8 Weeks (≈90 Hours)

Engine:

Creation Kit

Language Used:

Papyrus

Role(s):

Quest Designer

“McConnell Manor” is a singleplayer story-based quest mod for Fallout 4. This quest has the player investigating the disappearance of Thomas Sterett, a daring teenager who went missing after he entered the McConnell Manor in search of riches.

The McConnell Manor, located on the outskirts of Concord, has long since been abandoned but is still a notorious topic of the rumor mills of the area. Upon investigation of the manor, the player finds that the manor wasn’t as abandoned as they were previously made to believe as it is home to undead being, both freaky and friendly.

Design Goals

Branching Quest Design

Design a quest with one major narrative branch that influences the story and how characters interact with the player

Believable Old Manor

Create a believable abandoned manor that told a story that complemented the explicit narrative through environmental storytelling

Papyrus Scripting

Focus on doing more complex scripted pieces and creating unique functionality with Papyrus.

Quest Maps

Narrative Breakdown

For the "McConnell Manor" Quest, there is a narrative branch introduced in the parallel/interjecting quest "Ghost, Interrupted". The player must essentially decide the fate of Jedediah's stolen ring.

They first have the choice to take the ring from Thomas or the let him keep it.

If they make the choice to take it from him, they then have another choice to make: either return the ring to Jedediah or keep it for themselves.

Design Challenges

The Memento Puzzle

In order for the player to progress in the McConnell Manor, they must help the ghost of Jedediah McConnell return three items, which were moved by the child Thomas, to their original resting places. There was one memento for each family member that used to live in the house (excluding Jedediah) and they all had their own unique dresser that they needed to be returned to.

Major Challenge

Puzzle Conveyance was the biggest issue with the Memento Puzzle. I needed to provide players with enough information to solve the puzzle without explicitly giving away the solution.

Initial Implementation

Originally, the player could only ask Jedediah for help and Jedediah would give one clue about each of his family members. This gave the player enough information to know where each resident's item might be, but not necessarily where it would go.

Solution

To be sure players had enough diegetic information to solve the puzzle I did two things. The first was simply naming each dresser. The second was writing additional notes for each family member that I placed in their specific dressers.

This way, the player would see the notes when they investigated the dressers. So if they found the notes before they found the mementos or after, they would have enough information regardless to determine whose memento was whose.

Jedediah's Emotion Problem

Early feedback that I got from playtests was that it didn’t seem like Jedediah really cared about his family members.

Major Issues

Jedediah’s initial dialogue didn’t include language that invoked a sense of love and for his family members. For example, in the original dialogue he referred to their prized possessions as simply “things”.

There was also a lack of dialogue options in Jedediah’s conversations that allowed the player to hear Jedediah talk about each individual family member. The player could ask Jedediah for help with the memento puzzle by inquiring about each member and he would tell them one thing that would give the player key information about that family member in connection to a memento. But besides these three options, the player couldn’t speak to Jedediah to learn how he felt about his family.

Solution

I created a book called “Jedediah’s Journal” that chronicled events from the passing of his wife up until his death. This included entries that detailed why and how each of his three family members came to live with him in his home. Because a journal or a diary is usually a very personal private item for a person, I wrote the journal entries to be Jedediah writing from a very open and honest place.

This journal, which is placed in a position where the player is very likely to happen upon it and read it, showed more of Jedediah’s relationship with his family and helped players empathize with him.
