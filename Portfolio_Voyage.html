Programming Work

Audio Systems

For Voyage, my audio responsibilities include editing and implementing sound effects. This required me to program triggers for sound effects in the game's other C# scripts.

I created audio controller scripts for game objects such as the sailor boat, the sailors, the chasing ghost ship, and the boss ship that had more extensive audio needs than just a one off sounds played on death or impact.

I also mixed the audio using Unity's audio mixer and implemented functionality that allowed the player to mute both music and sound effects separately from the settings menu.

Level Design Work

The Tutorial Level

The goal of this level was to create a tutorial level to introduce the player to the majority of the game's mechanics in an intuitive manner. Those mechanics included shooting gems to collect them, catching sailors by cutting their rope with the harpoon gun, killing enemies (both by shooting them directly and with falling stalagmites), and outrunning the chasing ghost ship.

The first section consists of stalagmites with gems contained within to get the player used to collecting gems and show they that they can also be damaged by the stalagmites.

The section with the horizontal platform contains stalagmites that fall when the player gets close. These stalagmites get blocked by the platforms (so they don't hurt the player) but the player must adjust their speed to speed up and slow down to catch the subsequently falling gems.

The next section allows the player to get used to catching sailors without any danger. And the subsequent section is all about killing bats, both directly and with falling stalagmites.

Post Mortem

What Went Well
  • Ability to communicate with team members: everyone was open to having discussions and I felt comfortable being able to voice an opinion that might've been different from the group consensus
  • All my team members were very competent in their individual disciplines, so when I was onboarded to the team, everything was already progressing pretty well and it was easy to jump in and immediately start contributing
  • We were able to hit all of our milestone deliverables
What Went Wrong
  • Accurately tracking hours for scrum board tasks: frequently we came to end of milestones and would find that we were missing or over on hours
  • Daily Scrum Buy-In: There were some days where some team members felt the process wasn't beneficial for just a small team of 5
  • Sometimes it was hard to come to a consensus on what to do when we were getting conflicting playtester feedback about the game’s difficulty
What I Learned
  • Improved team communication and work skills
    • Learned when and how to concede and compromise on design decisions
    • Learned how to speak up with conflicting opinions and have productive discussions
  • I became very comfortable using perforce and learned how to use it on a team project without creating merge conflicts
